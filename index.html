This plot shows the energy difference of the ground state and the energy to have the two protons some radius apart.

The blue curve shows the energy needed for a bonding system to form. This shows that at some radius R0 it will settle as the molecule always wants to go to the lowest energy level.

The orange curve shows the energy needed for an antibonding system to form. This curve shows that there is no radius at which it will settle and instead that they will want to continue to drift forever.

This plot shows the probability that the electron will be in a particular space for a system in superposition between the bonding and antibding states.

The red dots represent the nuclei/protons in the H2+ system.

The two constants c1 and c2 represent the superposition state when c1 is the bonding state and c2 is the antibonding state.

The graph shows the probability that the electron will be in that slice of space given respect to the nuclei/protons (red dots), the superposition in bonding and antibonding state, and time. We can see that as you change the superposition state to be either 100% or 100% antibonding that it will represent each of the respective graphs regardless of time.

This plot shows the probability that the electron will be in a particular space for a bonding system where the two orbitals of the nuclei/protons constructively interfere.

The red dots represent the nuclei/protons in the H2+ system.

The blue graph represents the probability that the electron will be in that slice of space given respect to the nuclei/protons (red dots) for the bonding system. We can see that there is some probability that the electron will be inbetween the two nulei 'showing' the constructive interference and that they will bond.

This plot shows the probability that the electron will be in a particular space for an antibonding system where the two orbitals of the nuclei/protons destructively interfere.

The red dots represent the nuclei/protons in the H2+ system.

The orange graph represents the probability that the electron will be in that slice of space given respect to the nuclei/protons (red dots) for the antibonding system. We can see that there is 0 probability that the electron will be inbetween the two nulei 'showing' the destructive interference and that they will not bond.
